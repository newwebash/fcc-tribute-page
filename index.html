Michelle Obama

Michelle Obama was the First Lady of the United States during Barack Obama's presidency from 2009-2017. She is widely respected and celebrated as an activist for civil rights. As FLOTUS, she promoted initiatives to educate youth about healthy eating and support veterans and their families.

A timeline of Michelle Obama's successes

Source: Wikipedia

  • 1985 - Graduated cum laude with a Bachelor of Arts from Princeton University.
  • 1988 - Earned her J.D. from Harvard Law School.
  • 1991 - Transitioned from position as Attorney to the public sector.
  • 1993 - Became Executive Director for the Chicago office of Public Allies.
  • 2002 - Began working for University of Chicago Hospitals.
  • 2008 - Became First Lady of the United States.
  • 2010 - Undertook "Let's Move!" iniitative to reverse trend of childhood obesity in the United States.
  • 2012 - Was awarded the Jerald Washington Memorial Founders' Award by the National Coalition for Homeless Veterans (NCHV).
  • 2018 - Release her memoir, "Becoming".
  • 2020 - Launched The Michelle Obama podcast.
